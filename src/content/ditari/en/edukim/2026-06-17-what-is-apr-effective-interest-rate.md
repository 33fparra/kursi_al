A few years ago, I saw my parents arguing very intensely at home. Even though it is normal for couples to have disagreements, this time my mother was genuinely furious. She kept repeating loudly: *\"But how can it be 350 euros? How? Where on earth did this come from? How is it possible that you didn't notice it at all?\"*.

It turned out my father, who had zero experience or knowledge in managing bank accounts, had unknowingly applied for and activated an **overdraft** (account deficit line) and completely forgot to repay it. So, the absolute second someone transferred money into his account, it vanished instantly. My mother initially assumed he had secretly spent those funds on something else, but when she discovered it was actually a silent debt that had been growing without him realizing it at all, she became even more upset.

That was the very first time in my life I ever heard the term overdraft.

In Albania and Kosovo, banks market this service to citizens as an exclusive perk for \"VIP clients,\" with a supposedly low interest rate. But the naked truth is that the bank is turning you into an addict of this product while hiding its actual price tag: the **APR** indicator (which in official local contracts is always written under its native acronym: **NEI - Norma Efektive e Interesit**). APR and NEI are the exact same mathematical metric; APR is simply the international English term for what our local laws label as NEI.

---

## Why is an Overdraft a \"Vampire\" Credit Line?

When a bank offers to activate an overdraft, the consultant paints the scenario as a completely harmless safety cushion: *\"We are letting you spend up to two monthly salaries in advance in case you encounter an emergency, and we only calculate a nominal interest rate of 10% per year\"*. On the surface, that sounds highly reasonable, right?

The massive catch that no one breaks down for you at the teller counter is that the interest on a negative balance is not calculated like a normal loan or a fixed installment plan. An overdraft is a credit instrument calculated on a daily basis. Every single night, while you are fast asleep, the bank's software system checks exactly how many Lekë or Euros you owe at that precise split second and applies the interest percentage over that volume.

If you add the annual loan origination or contract renewal fee, the monthly current account maintenance fees, and the mandatory tied insurance policies to that baseline daily interest, that beautiful 10% advertisement rate transforms into an **actual APR (NEI) hovering between 15% and 18% per year**. You are paying a high-interest usury rate, masked as a corporate favor or privilege.

---

## The Terrifying Math: What an Account Deficit Costs You After 60 Days and 6 Months

To stop viewing a negative balance as \"safety funds\" and to understand the real capital you are throwing away every day, let's run a calculation that will leave you speechless. Let's assume you have gone through a rough couple of months and have maxed out your overdraft limit to a total value of **150,000 Lekë** (or **€1,500** if you live in Kosovo).

If you fail to fill that financial hole and leave the account frozen in negative territory, this is the approximate cost that the bank will siphon out of your pocket completely silently:

* **After 60 days in the negative:** The bank is not going to wait until the end of the year to collect its money. In just two months, the accumulated daily interest plus the automated account fees will cost you roughly **4,800 Lekë** (€50) extra. Your debt is no longer 150.000; it has risen to 154,800 Lekë, shrinking your purchasing power for the following month without you buying anything.
* **After 6 months (180 days) in the negative:** This is where the snowball becomes destructive for household finances. If you spend half a year at the bottom of the pit without ever bringing the balance back to positive ground, the real APR chokes everything out. Between daily compound interest, the contract renewal fee automatically applied mid-year, and prolonged deficit penalties, the cost flies to almost **16,500 Lekë** (around €170).


| Days with a negative balance | Your initial debt | Accumulated interest and fees | Total owed to the bank |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **30 days** | 150,000 Lekë / €1,500 | ~2,100 Lekë / €22 | **152,100 Lekë / €1,522** |
| **60 days** | 150,000 Lekë / €1,500 | ~4.800 Lekë / €50 | **154,800 Lekë / €1.550** |
| **6 months (180 days)** | 150,000 Lekë / €1,500 | ~16,500 Lekë / €170 | **166,500 Lekë / €1,670** |


<br>

Look closely at that final number! In just six months, for the simple \"privilege\" of having a negative account balance, you have gifted the bank the equivalent of half a year's electricity bills, a whole month's grocery shopping, or a weekend getaway. You are paying the bank the price of a brand-new television set just for the right to be broke.

## How to Break the Chains and Exit This Vicious Cycle

The true danger of an overdraft is not just the amount of money you lose to interest; it is the psychological impact it inflicts on you. You get accustomed to living in the financial underground. On day one you pay off the deficit, ending up completely broke, and by day five you have to tap the overdraft again just to buy basic groceries. You turn into a modern slave working for free for the bank during the first week of the month.

The only way to win this game is to stop playing. If you have an active overdraft, put together an emergency plan: trim your expenses to the bare minimum for three months, use cash if necessary to break the habit of digital spending, and push your account until it hits positive zero. The moment you see that on day one your salary remains entirely in your pocket instead of going to pay off the ghosts of last month, you will have regained total control over your life.

## 🧠 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

<details>
<summary><b>Can the bank cancel my overdraft without notifying me at all?</b></summary>
Yes. An overdraft is a short-term, revolving credit line that banks review periodically. If risk analysts notice that your monthly income has dropped, that you have switched employers, or that your credit history in the Credit Registry at the Bank of Albania shows warning signs, they hold the full legal right to lower your limit or shut down the service immediately, demanding full repayment of the negative balance within the day.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>What is the actual difference between nominal overdraft interest and the APR?</b></summary>
Nominal interest is the raw baseline percentage the bank calculates strictly for the days and the volume you pulled out (for example, 10%). The APR (or NEI) is the final, real, and total cost of this setup because, in addition to that 10%, it packages contract renewal fees, current account maintenance fees, and any other mandatory insurance policy that accompanies the line.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Does constantly remaining in the negative affect my credit score?</b></summary>
Staying within the authorized overdraft limit does not trigger a direct penalty if you respect the criteria. However, if you overrun the agreed limit or if you spend consecutive months without ever bringing the balance to a positive figure, local bank risk algorithms will classify you as a high-risk client, severely hindering your approval for a mortgage or a business loan in the future.
</details>
