When I arrived in Albania with my wife, one thing left us completely breathless from day one: the amount of people who smoke. Of course, in South America, where I come from, people smoke too, but it tends to be a very small part of the population and restricted to specific spaces. Here, however, the visual impression is that almost everyone has a cigarette in hand, regardless of age or time of day. Tobacco in this country seems to be a life companion just as common as coffee itself.

This afternoon, chatting with the owner of the small supermarket near my house, I got curious and asked about prices and consumption. She explained that each pack ranges between **250 and 400 lekë**, with American brands being the most expensive. But what really opened my eyes was when I asked if it was normal for people to smoke a whole pack a day. She laughed and said, "One is nothing! So many people smoke two, and some even get through three packs a day."

As she handed me my change, she cracked a joke that felt like a perfect X-ray of the local reality: *"Here in Albania, we might run out of money for food, but never for cigarettes."*

---

## Tobacco Math vs. A Real Salary

If we bring our feet back to earth and look at the numbers for this year 2026, the mathematics behind this habit is alarming. As we analyzed before, the net salary of an average worker in the country hovers between 600 and 800 euros per month (around 70,000 lekë).

Taking an intermediate price of **300 lekë** (about 3 euros) per pack, the daily total quickly turns into a snowball that suffocates any family budget. Look at how the impact shifts depending on the number of daily packs:

* **1 pack a day:** That is 300 lekë daily. Over a month, it adds up to about 9,000 lekë (around 90 euros). This represents **12.8%** of a 700-euro salary.
* **2 packs a day:** That is 600 lekë daily. Over a month, the figure jumps to 18,000 lekë (around 180 euros). We are talking about **25.7%** of the total salary.
* **3 packs a day:** The extreme case, but a real one. That is 900 lekë daily, which translates to 27,000 lekë a month (around 270 euros). Nearly **38.5%** of everything you earn turns into smoke!

## The Clean Math: When Smoke Smothers Your Wallet

Let's be blunt: putting out the cigarette is not easy, least of all in a country where lighting up is almost a reflex action the second you sit down on any terrace. But if the health warnings on the pack no longer phase you, try a purely mathematical exercise. Look at it as a truce for your wallet before it becomes a truce for your lungs. 

Burning **25% or 38% of your salary** every single month means you are working from Monday to Wednesday exclusively to bankroll tobacco companies. And the darkest part of that supermarket owner's joke is that if money for food is tight today because of the smoke, tomorrow it will be tight to pay for medicines or hospital bills when your body demands accounts. In the Albania of 2026, tossing the lighter is not just a favor to your health; it is the most profitable short-term investment you can make to safeguard your future.
