Thousands of years ago, someone observed something that others didn't see. Two rocks collided, a spark appeared, and after many attempts, controlled fire was born. It wasn't just a curious discovery; it was an advantage for whoever learned to control it. Whoever mastered fire could scare away wild animals, cook food, withstand the cold, and keep doing things after the sun went down. Fire didn't make humans smarter overnight, but it did multiply the strength of those who learned to use it first.

Today, in this year 2026, we are living through the exact same thing. Artificial Intelligence (AI) has become the new fire of our time.

For many people here in Albania, when they hear the words \"Artificial Intelligence,\" they still picture a science fiction movie. They think of *Terminator*, *Robocop*, or metal robots with red flashing lights coming from the future to destroy the world. But the reality is very different: AI is already here, and it is embedded in our phones, in the computers we use, in banks, and even in the corner shops.

Many people already use it for very simple things: asking for an internet translation, summarizing a long text, or making a funny image to send over WhatsApp. But that is like sitting next to a bonfire that someone else lit just to warm your hands. The real advantage appears when you learn to light that bonfire yourself. In other words, when a worker learns to use AI to complete their office tasks in half the time, analyze their shop's sales, draft documents, or solve problems much faster than anyone else.

History teaches us that new technologies do not wipe out people entirely; what they do is reward those who learn to use them before the rest. This is changing the money map worldwide. For example, for many, many years, Japan's most valuable company was Toyota, the car brand. It was the great symbol of traditional factories. However, it was recently surpassed by SoftBank, a business group that grew tremendously because it invested heavily in Artificial Intelligence and the microchips that computers need to think.

If the world's money is moving toward that sector, what is happening on our streets here in Albania?

## From Tirana's Offices to Laws with the European Union

In Albania, AI is no longer just a theory from the movies. In fact, our country has become one of the places in the region where this technology is being tested fastest within the government itself—this doesn't mean it's being done perfectly, but the attempt is definitely being made.

The Albanian State has started using Artificial Intelligence at the highest levels of administration. The plan for this year 2026 is to use advanced systems to translate and adapt the thousands upon thousands of pages of laws required for Albania to join the European Union. Doing this with humans would take years of paperwork and offices full of folders; AI can read, translate, and compare those texts in just a few seconds.

Furthermore, in portals like e-Albania, smart virtual assistants are being integrated so that citizens can complete their administrative tasks directly, without having to deal with an official behind a counter. This helps make processes faster and, above all, reduces the opportunities for favoritism or corruption.

## Are You One of Those Who Watches the Fire, or One of Those Who Knows How to Light It?

For an average worker in Albania, who today has to stretch a salary of between 600 and 800 euros a month to pay for rent and food, knowing how to use these tools can change their future.

Think about *Call Centers*. For more than ten years, answering calls in Italian, English, or German was the lifesaver job for thousands of young Albanians. Nowadays, AI voice systems handle clients and solve their doubts by speaking in an almost human way. Traditional operator positions are declining, but much better-paying jobs are emerging for people who know how to control, correct, and improve those smart systems.

Look at the day-to-day difference between remaining a spectator or taking the initiative:

* **The user who only warms themselves by the fire:** Copies and pastes a long text given by the AI without changing a single word, making their work look boring, repetitive, and mechanical.
* **The one who knows how to light the bonfire:** Uses AI to complete boring office reports in five minutes and uses the rest of the day to propose new ideas to their boss.
* **The local business that falls behind:** Keeps uploading simple photos to social media, expecting people to come and buy by pure chance.
* **The business that moves forward:** Uses simple AI tools to understand which days of the week sell the most and what products people prefer based on the weather or the month.

## The Advantage of Learning First

The best thing about Artificial Intelligence in today's Albania is that it doesn't care about \"connections\" or insider acquaintances. In a job market where moving forward has often depended on who you know or having friends in politics, AI breaks those chains. An international company in Germany, England, or the United States looking for a remote worker doesn't care what family you come from or what party you belong to; they care if you know how to solve a problem quickly using technology.

Learning to use these tools intelligently is the grand opportunity for young Albanians to bypass the hurdles of the local market, earn international salaries, and build a good life from Tirana, Durrës, or Shkodër, without ever having to buy a plane ticket to leave the country.

The fire is already here, on the coffee shop table right next to the espresso. The choice belongs to each individual: are we going to wait until the technology is so common that it no longer gives us any advantage, or are we going to learn to master the spark today?
